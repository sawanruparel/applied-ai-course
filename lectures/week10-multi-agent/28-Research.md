# Key Research Papers

Multi-agent AI systems are an active area of research. These papers provide the foundations for the patterns we covered today.

| Paper | Authors | Year | Key Contribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| [**AutoGen**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.08155) | Wu et al. (Microsoft) | 2023 | Conversation-driven multi-agent framework; introduced the concept of "conversable agents" that collaborate through chat |
| [**CAMEL**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17760) | Li et al. | 2023 | Role-playing framework where agents take on personas and collaborate through structured dialogue; introduced inception prompting |
| [**MetaGPT**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.00352) | Hong et al. | 2023 | Software engineering multi-agent system with SOPs; agents follow standardized operating procedures like a real dev team |
| [**AgentVerse**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.10848) | Chen et al. | 2023 | Dynamic agent team composition; studies how to assemble the right group of agents for a given task |
| [**ChatDev**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.07924) | Qian et al. | 2023 | End-to-end software development using a "virtual software company" of agents (CEO, CTO, programmer, tester, reviewer) |
| [**Voyager**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.16291) | Wang et al. (NVIDIA) | 2023 | Lifelong learning agent in Minecraft; demonstrates skill library accumulation across agent iterations |
| [**Self-Refine**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17651) | Madaan et al. | 2023 | Iterative self-improvement without external feedback; foundational work for the reflection pattern |
| [**Tree of Thoughts**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.10601) | Yao et al. | 2023 | Deliberate reasoning through exploring multiple solution paths; related to competitive/debate patterns |

## Recommended Reading Order
1. [**AutoGen**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.08155) — Foundational framework paper; covers most patterns
2. [**Self-Refine**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17651) — Simple and powerful; the basis for reflection
3. [**MetaGPT**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.00352) — Best example of hierarchical multi-agent with structured roles
4. [**CAMEL**](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17760) — Understanding role-playing and collaborative dynamics

## Stay Current
- Multi-agent research moves fast — new papers weekly on arXiv
- Follow the `cs.AI` and `cs.MA` (multi-agent systems) categories
- Framework documentation often outpaces published papers
