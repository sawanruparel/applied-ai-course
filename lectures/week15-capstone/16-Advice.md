# Parting Advice for AI Practitioners

### Build, Don't Just Read
- Papers and blog posts give you vocabulary. Building gives you intuition.
- Ship something small every month. A weekend MCP server teaches more than a week of tutorials.
- The gap between "I understand transformers" and "I deployed an agent" is where careers are made.

### Stay Close to the Problem
- The best AI engineers spend 50% of their time understanding the domain, not the model.
- Talk to users. Watch them struggle. The problem definition matters more than the architecture.
- Resist the urge to use the newest model — use the right model (Week 12).

### Evaluate Relentlessly
- If you can't measure it, you can't improve it (Week 7).
- Build eval suites before you build features. Test-driven AI development.
- Be skeptical of your own demos. Adversarial testing is a habit, not a phase (Week 14).

### Invest in Fundamentals
- Prompting is not going away — it's becoming more important as models get more capable.
- Understanding tokenization, context windows, and inference costs saves you from expensive mistakes.
- The AI stack changes every 6 months. Fundamentals change every 6 years. Invest accordingly.

**The tools will change. The thinking won't.**
