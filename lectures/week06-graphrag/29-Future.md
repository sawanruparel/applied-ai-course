# Where the Field Is Heading

- **Cost collapse** — LazyGraphRAG, LinearRAG, and E2GraphRAG are converging on near-vector-RAG indexing costs while preserving graph benefits
- **Hybrid-first architectures** — the consensus is shifting from "graph OR vector" to "graph AND vector" as the default production pattern
- **Agentic GraphRAG** — agents that can dynamically construct, query, and update knowledge graphs as part of multi-step reasoning workflows
- **Incremental graph updates** — moving from full re-indexing to incremental entity and relationship updates as documents change
- **Standardized evaluation** — GraphRAG-Bench and similar efforts will establish common benchmarks, accelerating reproducible research
- **Domain-specific ontologies** — pre-built graph schemas for healthcare, legal, finance that reduce the "cold start" problem of entity extraction
- **Multimodal knowledge graphs** — incorporating images, tables, and structured data alongside text into unified graph representations

> The next 12 months will likely see GraphRAG move from "interesting research" to "standard production pattern" for complex enterprise RAG.
