# The Open-Weight Landscape

## Llama 4 (Meta)

- Shipped **April 2026** as a Mixture-of-Experts family
- **Scout:** 17B active / 109B total params; **Maverick:** 17B active / 400B total params
- Sparse MoE keeps inference cheap while raising total capacity
- Massive ecosystem: fine-tunes, quantizations, tooling
- Foundation for thousands of derivative models

## Qwen 3.5 / 3.6 (Alibaba)

- **Qwen 3.5 (397B)** and **Qwen 3.6 (27B)** shipped March–May 2026 under **Apache 2.0**
- Qwen 3.6 hits **77.2% on SWE-bench** despite its compact 27B size
- Strong multilingual performance (especially CJK)
- Coder-specialized variants remain a standout strength
- Competitive with the top open-weight models at equivalent sizes

## Mistral / Mixtral

- Mixtral 8x22B: MoE architecture, efficient inference
- Mistral Large: closed-weight flagship
- Strong European AI alternative
- Pioneered efficient MoE for open models

## DeepSeek (open-weight reasoning)

- **DeepSeek V3.2 (January 2026):** outperforms GPT-5 on several reasoning tasks while staying open-weight
- Keeps frontier-class reasoning fully downloadable and self-hostable
- **DeepSeek R2** (anticipated 1.2T-param MoE, ~78B active) has **not yet shipped** as of May 2026
- Continues the lineage that made open reasoning models mainstream

## Phi (Microsoft)

- Phi-3/Phi-4: "small language models" (3.8B-14B)
- Trained on high-quality synthetic + curated data
- Punches well above weight class on reasoning
- Ideal for edge deployment and fine-tuning
