# Key Papers and Readings

| Paper / Resource | Year | Why It Matters |
|-----------------|------|----------------|
| [Attention Is All You Need (Vaswani et al.)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762) | 2017 | The transformer architecture that started it all |
| [Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models (Kaplan et al.)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2001.08361) | 2020 | Established training-time scaling laws |
| [Chain-of-Thought Prompting (Wei et al.)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903) | 2022 | Showed reasoning emerges from step-by-step prompting |
| [Training Compute-Optimal LLMs (Hoffmann et al., Chinchilla)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.15556) | 2022 | Optimal balance of model size and training data |
| [GPT-4 Technical Report (OpenAI)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.08774) | 2023 | Frontier multimodal model capabilities |
| [Scaling LLM Test-Time Compute (Snell et al.)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.03314) | 2024 | Formal framework for inference-time scaling |
| [DeepSeek-R1 Technical Report](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.12948) | 2025 | Open-weight reasoning via RL, emergent CoT |
| [OpenAI o1 System Card](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.16720) | 2024 | Safety analysis of reasoning models |
| [The Shift from Models to Compound AI Systems (Zaharia et al.)](https://bair.berkeley.edu/blog/2024/02/18/compound-ai-systems/) | 2024 | Why systems beat individual models |
