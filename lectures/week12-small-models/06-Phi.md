# Microsoft Phi-3 and Phi-4

## The "Textbook Quality" Thesis

- Core insight: data quality matters more than data quantity at small scale
- Phi models trained on heavily curated, synthetic, and textbook-style data
- Result: dramatically better performance per parameter than expected

## Phi-3 (April 2024)

- **Phi-3 mini (3.8B)**: rivals Mixtral 8x7B and GPT-3.5 on many benchmarks
- **Phi-3 small (7B)** and **Phi-3 medium (14B)**: competitive with much larger models
- 128K context window support
- Optimized for ONNX Runtime — fast inference on CPU and mobile

## Phi-4 (Dec 2024 – Feb 2025)

- **Phi-4 (14B)**: outperforms GPT-4o on MATH and GPQA benchmarks
- **Phi-4 mini (3.8B)**: a benchmark-beating edge model — runs at 15–20 tok/s on an M1 MacBook Air at Q4_K_M, matches GPT-4o on structured-extraction benchmarks, and fits on entry-level hardware
- Heavy use of synthetic data generated by larger models
- Particularly strong at STEM reasoning and structured tasks

## Trade-offs

- Narrower training data means weaker on open-ended creative tasks
- Less robust on adversarial/safety benchmarks compared to Llama/Qwen
- Microsoft Research license — check terms for commercial use
- Best suited for STEM, structured extraction, and reasoning pipelines
